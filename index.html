Why?

Homelessness is a prevalent issue here in Stockton . With the addiction of drinking commonly seen among the homeless population 38% abuse of alcohol malnutrition is even more common. Excessive drinking leads to poor absorption of nutrients which are already scarce in the processed foods they eat. Processed foods are a go to among low-income individuals as they are often the most “filling” and cheapest of groceries. Supplying the community with resources that provide fresh and nutritious foods was a goal of mine. Giving out these seedling if maintained correctly will supply students with their own free resource they can continue to work and receive output from.



 
 
 
 

DIY

Recycle anything that can be turned into containers for your seeds to create your own garden. Whether it is a bottle, pot, tire anything can be reused to hold your new plants. Help the environment by recycling and help yourself by growing your own nutritious veggies
